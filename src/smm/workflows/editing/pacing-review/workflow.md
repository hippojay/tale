# Editorial Pacing Review Workflow

## Objective
Analyze and optimize story pacing from editorial perspective.

## Prerequisites
- Manuscript to review
- Understanding of genre pacing norms

## Steps

### 1. Macro Pacing Assessment
- Overall story rhythm
- Act length proportions
- Major beat spacing
- Tension arc shape

### 2. Chapter-Level Analysis
For each chapter:
- Length appropriate?
- Internal pacing?
- Hook effectiveness?
- Cliffhanger strength?

### 3. Scene-Level Analysis
- Scene length variety
- Scene type distribution
- Transition smoothness
- White space usage

### 4. Identify Pacing Problems

**Too Slow:**
- Excessive description
- Redundant scenes
- Over-explained emotions
- Unnecessary backstory
- Circular dialogue

**Too Fast:**
- Rushed emotional beats
- Skipped transitions
- Underdeveloped scenes
- Missing reactions
- Compressed climax

### 5. Tension Mapping
- Where does tension peak?
- Where does it valley?
- Is pattern intentional?
- Does it serve story?

### 6. Reader Engagement Check
- Where might reader skim?
- Where might reader put book down?
- Where is reader compelled forward?

### 7. Genre Pacing Comparison
- Does pacing match genre expectations?
- Thriller: fast, relentless
- Literary: measured, reflective
- Romance: emotional beats paced
- Mystery: revelation timing

### 8. Recommendations
- Scenes to expand (rushed)
- Scenes to compress (slow)
- Scenes to cut (unnecessary)
- Beats to add (missing)
- Reordering suggestions

## Output
- Pacing review report
- Specific revision recommendations
