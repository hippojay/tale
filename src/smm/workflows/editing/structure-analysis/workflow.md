# Editorial Structure Analysis Workflow

## Objective
Deep dive into story structure and plot architecture for developmental editing.

## Prerequisites
- Manuscript or detailed outline
- Story Bible

## Steps

### 1. Reverse-Engineer Structure
- Map what exists (not what was planned)
- Identify actual act breaks
- Find actual plot points
- Locate actual midpoint and climax

### 2. Compare to Intent
- Does structure match SRD intent?
- Where did story diverge?
- Is divergence improvement or problem?

### 3. Evaluate Foundation
- Is premise clear?
- Is central question compelling?
- Are stakes established early?
- Is hook effective?

### 4. Act-by-Act Analysis
**Act One:**
- Setup efficiency
- Character introduction
- World establishment
- Inciting incident timing
- First plot point clarity

**Act Two:**
- Complication variety
- Escalation pattern
- Midpoint impact
- Subplot integration
- Dark moment depth

**Act Three:**
- Climax preparation
- Climax execution
- Resolution completeness
- Emotional satisfaction

### 5. Plot Thread Tracking
- Main plot coherence
- Subplot resolution
- Foreshadowing payoff
- Chekhov's guns fired

### 6. Causality Check
- Does A lead to B?
- Are transitions logical?
- Are coincidences minimized?
- Is protagonist active?

### 7. Identify Structural Problems
- Missing beats
- Misplaced beats
- Redundant scenes
- Pacing issues
- Logic gaps

### 8. Recommendations
- Scenes to add
- Scenes to cut
- Scenes to move
- Beats to strengthen
- Restructuring options

## Output
- Structure analysis report
- Structural revision recommendations
