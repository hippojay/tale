# Song Sheet Workflow

## Objective
Create a complete per-song reference document that captures the dramatic function, craft specifications, and lyric draft for a single song. The Song Sheet is the living record of one number — updated as the lyric evolves.

## Prerequisites
- Song Brief from the Dialogue-to-Song workflow (or equivalent information)
- Musical Bible (musical-bible.md) if available — to check song list and placement
- Character profiles for all singing characters

## Steps

### 1. Confirm Song Identity
- Confirm working title, act/scene position, and character(s)
- Cross-check against the Song Placement Map in the Musical Structure document
- Verify this song does not duplicate the function of another planned number

### 2. Establish Dramatic Specifications
Document the following from the Song Brief:
- **Primary Song Trigger**: which of the 10 triggers applies
- **Song Voice**: Internal/Soliloquy | External/Direct | Mixed
- **Emotional Arc**: emotional state at start → emotional state at end
- **Dramatic Function**: what the song does to the story (escalates / reveals / commits / grieves / decides / connects / crystallises theme)
- **What changes**: state explicitly what is different in character, relationship, or audience understanding by the final note

### 3. Establish Craft Specifications
- **Song Type**: opening_number / i_want_song / love_song / charm_song / comedy_number / duet / ensemble_number / narrative_exposition / act_one_finale / eleven_oclock_number / reprised_theme
- **Proposed Song Form**: Verse-A-A-B-A (Broadway standard) | Verse-Chorus | Through-Composed | Other
- **Tone**: intimate ballad | uptempo comic | anthemic ensemble | rock/pop | wry character song | lament | charm song | other
- **Reference Songs**: 1-3 touchstones from the canon that capture the intended feel or approach
- **Key Image or Metaphor**: any central image, object, or conceit to anchor the lyric
- **Prosody Notes**: any known rhythmic or stress considerations (e.g., character has a speech pattern; lyric must fit a specific meter)

### 4. Write or Capture the Lyric Draft
Using the Song Brief and craft specifications, write or paste the current lyric draft with structural labels:

```
VERSE
[verse lyric here]

A1
[first A section]

A2
[second A section — variation or development of A1]

BRIDGE / RELEASE
[B section — the emotional breakthrough or turn; different melodic and harmonic territory]

A3
[final A section — lands with new meaning after the Bridge]

CODA (if applicable)
[final lines that close the song]
```

For verse-chorus form:
```
VERSE 1
[lyric]

CHORUS
[lyric]

VERSE 2
[lyric]

CHORUS
[lyric]

BRIDGE
[lyric]

CHORUS (FINAL)
[lyric — often with variation for the emotional landing]
```

### 5. Reprise Connection
- If this song has or is a reprise: note the original and what the lyric change reveals
- If this song may be reprised later: flag which line or phrase has reprise potential and what new meaning it could carry

### 6. Status Tracking
Track the song's development:
- [ ] Brief complete
- [ ] First draft lyric written
- [ ] Prosody checked
- [ ] Character voice verified
- [ ] Dramaturgical arc confirmed (changes from start to end)
- [ ] Rhyme quality checked
- [ ] Integrated with surrounding book scenes
- [ ] Final lyric approved

## Output
- Complete Song Sheet document saved to `{output_folder}/musical-theatre/song-sheets/[song-title]-song-sheet.md`
- Song Sheet registered in Musical Bible song list (if musical-bible.md exists)
