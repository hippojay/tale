# Dialogue Review Workflow

## Objective
Specialized review of dialogue authenticity and effectiveness.

## Prerequisites
- Content with dialogue to review
- Character voice profiles from Story Bible

## Steps

### 1. Purpose Check
For each dialogue exchange:
- What's the purpose?
- Does it advance plot/character/relationship?
- Could it be cut?

### 2. Voice Distinction
- Can you identify speaker without tags?
- Do characters sound different?
- Are voices consistent with profiles?

### 3. Authenticity Check
- Does it sound natural?
- Is it how people actually talk?
- Appropriate contractions/fragments?

### 4. Subtext Evaluation
- Is there meaning beneath words?
- Are characters saying what they mean?
- Is conflict present in conversation?

### 5. Information Delivery
- Avoiding "as you know, Bob"?
- Information woven naturally?
- Not too on-the-nose?

### 6. Pacing Review
- Exchanges appropriate length?
- Balance with action beats?
- Rhythm varied?

### 7. Tag Assessment
- Minimal tags used?
- "Said" preferred?
- Action beats instead where possible?

### 8. Common Problems
Check for:
- Monologuing
- Identical voices
- Exposition dumps
- Unrealistic formality
- Missing conflict

### 9. Recommendations
- Lines to cut
- Lines to revise
- Voice adjustments
- Subtext opportunities

## Output
- Dialogue review report
- Specific revision suggestions
