# Reader Experience Workflow

## Objective
Evaluate chapter from reader engagement perspective.

## Prerequisites
- Chapter to evaluate
- Genre expectations

## Steps

### 1. First Impression
- Read as a reader, not editor
- Note emotional responses
- Mark where attention wanders
- Mark where engaged

### 2. Hook Evaluation
- Does opening grab?
- Would you keep reading?
- Is promise clear?

### 3. Engagement Tracking
Through chapter:
- Where do you want to skim?
- Where are you compelled forward?
- Where are you confused?
- Where are you bored?

### 4. Emotional Journey
- What emotions evoked?
- Are they intentional?
- Is emotional arc satisfying?
- Do you care about characters?

### 5. Clarity Check
- Is action clear?
- Is motivation understandable?
- Are transitions smooth?
- Any confusion points?

### 6. Satisfaction Assessment
- Does chapter deliver?
- Is ending satisfying (or appropriately unsatisfying)?
- Would you read next chapter?

### 7. Genre Expectations
- Does it feel like the genre?
- Are conventions met?
- Is reader getting what they came for?

### 8. Page-Turner Test
- Are there reasons to keep reading?
- Questions raised?
- Tension maintained?
- Stakes clear?

### 9. Recommendations
- Engagement improvements
- Clarity fixes
- Emotional enhancements
- Pacing adjustments

## Output
- Reader experience report
- Engagement map
- Improvement suggestions
