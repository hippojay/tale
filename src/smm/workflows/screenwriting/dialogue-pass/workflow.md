# Screenplay Dialogue Pass Workflow

## Objective
Polish screenplay dialogue for subtext, character voice, speakability, and economy.

## Steps

### 1. Read-Through
- Read all dialogue aloud (or mentally "hear" each character)
- Mark lines that sound written rather than spoken
- Flag speeches longer than 3 lines
- Note where characters sound interchangeable

### 2. Subtext Pass
- Identify scenes where characters say exactly what they mean — fix these
- Add layers: what do they want the other person to think vs. what they actually feel?
- Use misdirection: characters talk about one thing while meaning another
- Let silences and pauses carry weight (use `(beat)` sparingly but effectively)

### 3. Character Voice Differentiation
- Each character needs a distinct speech pattern:
  - Vocabulary level and word choice
  - Sentence length tendency (clipped vs. flowing)
  - Verbal habits or tics (used sparingly)
  - What they avoid saying (as revealing as what they say)
- Test: cover character names — can you tell who's speaking?

### 4. Economy Pass
- Cut greetings and pleasantries unless they reveal character
- Remove on-the-nose exposition ("As you know, Bob...")
- Trim redundant exchanges
- If a look or action can replace a line, cut the line
- Shorten long speeches into rapid exchanges where possible

### 5. Speakability Check
- Read every line aloud
- Flag tongue-twisters and awkward phrasing
- Ensure contractions are used naturally (people say "don't" not "do not")
- Check that rhythm matches the emotional beat of the scene
- Verify actors could deliver these lines naturally

### 6. Exposition Management
- Identify all exposition in dialogue
- For each piece: can it be shown visually instead?
- If it must be spoken, disguise it in conflict (characters argue about the information)
- Spread exposition across scenes — never dump it all at once
- Use the "Pope in the pool" technique: deliver exposition during something visually interesting

## Output
- Polished dialogue draft
- Dialogue notes (changes made and rationale)
- Character voice reference sheet
