# Pacing Analysis Workflow

## Objective
Review and optimize story pacing and rhythm.

## Prerequisites
- Chapters or manuscript to analyze
- Story structure defined

## Steps

### 1. Map Scene Types
Categorize each scene:
- **Action:** Physical conflict, chase, fight
- **Dialogue:** Conversation-driven
- **Introspection:** Internal reflection
- **Description:** World-building, setting
- **Revelation:** Information delivery

### 2. Create Pacing Map
Visual representation:
- High tension scenes
- Low tension scenes
- Scene length variation
- Chapter length variation

### 3. Analyze Rhythm
- Is there variety in scene types?
- Do high-tension scenes have breathing room after?
- Is tension generally escalating?
- Are quiet moments earned?

### 4. Check Momentum
- Where does story drag?
- Where does it rush?
- Are transitions smooth?
- Is reader engagement maintained?

### 5. Evaluate Key Moments
- Do important scenes get appropriate space?
- Are climactic moments not rushed?
- Are setup scenes not overlong?

### 6. Sentence-Level Rhythm
- Short sentences for tension
- Longer sentences for reflection
- Variety within scenes
- Paragraph length variation

### 7. Chapter-Level Pacing
- Chapter length consistency
- Cliffhanger placement
- Opening hook strength
- Scene count per chapter

### 8. Identify Issues
- Scenes that drag
- Scenes that rush
- Missing breathing room
- Tension plateaus
- Momentum killers

### 9. Recommendations
- Scenes to expand
- Scenes to compress
- Scenes to cut
- Scenes to add
- Reordering suggestions

## Output
- Pacing analysis report
- Pacing map visualization
- Specific recommendations
