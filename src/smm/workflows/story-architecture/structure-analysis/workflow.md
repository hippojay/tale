# Structure Analysis Workflow

## Objective
Deep analysis of story structure to identify strengths and weaknesses.

## Prerequisites
- Story structure or manuscript to analyze
- Genre identified

## Steps

### 1. Map Current Structure
- Identify act breaks
- Locate major plot points
- Find midpoint
- Mark climax and resolution

### 2. Evaluate Act One
- Does opening hook engage?
- Is normal world established?
- Is inciting incident compelling?
- Is first plot point clear?
- Is act length appropriate (20-25%)?

### 3. Evaluate Act Two
- Does tension escalate?
- Is midpoint impactful?
- Are complications sufficient?
- Is dark moment earned?
- Is act length appropriate (50%)?

### 4. Evaluate Act Three
- Does climax satisfy?
- Is resolution complete?
- Are threads tied up?
- Is pacing appropriate?
- Is act length appropriate (25%)?

### 5. Subplot Analysis
- Are subplots integrated?
- Do they enhance main plot?
- Are they resolved?
- Is balance appropriate?

### 6. Structural Issues
Common problems to check:
- Sagging middle
- Rushed ending
- Delayed inciting incident
- Missing midpoint shift
- Unearned climax
- Dangling threads

### 7. Recommendations
- Structural adjustments needed
- Scenes to add/remove/move
- Pacing modifications
- Plot point strengthening

### 8. Document Analysis
- Structure analysis report
- Visual structure map
- Prioritized action items

## Output
- Structure analysis report
- Recommendations for improvement
