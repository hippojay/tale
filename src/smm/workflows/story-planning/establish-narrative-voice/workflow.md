# Establish Narrative Voice Workflow

## Objective
Define and document the narrative voice and prose style for this specific project through guided elicitation and sample generation.

## Prerequisites
- Story Requirements Document (SRD) with genre and audience defined
- Understanding of project goals and target readers

## Steps

### 1. Voice Foundation Questions

Ask the author to define their voice preferences:

**Formality Level:**
- Professional/Literary — Sophisticated, elevated language
- Standard/Polished — Clear, confident, accessible
- Casual/Conversational — Relaxed, intimate, direct
- Experimental/Unconventional — Boundary-pushing, artistic

**Tone:**
- Serious, contemplative, weighty
- Balanced, varied by scene needs
- Light, humorous, playful
- Dark, cynical, edgy
- Warm, optimistic, hopeful

**Pacing & Rhythm:**
- Fast-paced — Short sentences, punchy, urgent
- Moderate — Varied rhythm, balanced flow
- Leisurely — Longer sentences, contemplative, immersive
- Mixed — Adapts to scene intensity

**Narrative Distance:**
- Close/Intimate — Deep POV, character's thoughts/feelings dominate
- Moderate — Balance between character and narrator
- Distant/Observational — More narrator presence, less interiority
- Omniscient — Narrator has distinct voice and commentary

**Description Density:**
- Sparse — Minimal description, focus on action/dialogue
- Selective — Description where it serves mood or character
- Rich — Detailed sensory immersion
- Lush — Elaborate, poetic, atmospheric

**Dialogue Style:**
- Naturalistic — How people actually talk (fragments, interruptions)
- Stylized — Polished, purposeful, thematic
- Minimal — Sparse dialogue, action/description dominant
- Dialogue-heavy — Conversations drive story

### 2. Genre & Audience Alignment

Review SRD and confirm:
- Does voice match genre expectations?
- Does sophistication level match target audience?
- Does tone serve the story's themes?
- Are there genre conventions to honor or subvert?

### 3. Comparative References

Ask author to identify 2-3 published works with similar voice:
- What do you admire about their prose?
- What specific techniques do they use?
- What would you do differently?
- Are there authors whose style you want to avoid?

### 4. Sample Passage Generation

Generate 3 sample passages (same scene, different voices):

**Sample A: [Voice Style 1]**
- Write 200-300 word passage
- Apply first voice option

**Sample B: [Voice Style 2]**
- Same scene, different voice approach
- Contrast with Sample A

**Sample C: [Voice Style 3]**
- Third variation
- Show range of possibilities

### 5. Author Selection & Refinement

Present samples to author:
- Which feels most natural?
- Which serves the story best?
- What to keep from each?
- What to adjust?

Refine based on feedback and generate final sample.

### 6. Document Voice Profile

Create comprehensive voice guide:

**Voice Identity:**
- One-sentence voice description
- Key characteristics (3-5 defining traits)
- Prose sophistication level (accessible/polished/literary/experimental)

**Target Quality Metrics:**
- **TTR (Type-Token Ratio):**
  - Accessible: 0.150 - 0.175
  - Polished: 0.175 - 0.200
  - Literary: 0.200 - 0.225
  - Experimental: 0.225+
- **Sentence Length:**
  - Thriller/Action: 12-18 words average
  - Commercial Fiction: 15-20 words average
  - Literary Fiction: 20-25 words average
  - Experimental: 25+ words average (or highly varied)
- **Sentence Complexity:** 70-80% complex/compound, 20-30% simple
- **Show vs. Tell:** 60-80% showing (varies by scene type)

**Sentence Structure:**
- Average sentence length (short/medium/long/varied)
- Complexity level (simple/compound/complex/mixed)
- Fragment usage (never/rare/occasional/frequent)
- Paragraph length patterns

**Word Choice:**
- Vocabulary level (accessible/standard/elevated/literary)
- Metaphor frequency (sparse/moderate/rich)
- Adjective density (minimal/balanced/lush)
- Specific words/phrases to favor or avoid

**POV Execution:**
- Interiority depth (how much internal thought?)
- Sensory focus (which senses emphasized?)
- Emotional transparency (show/tell balance)
- Narrative distance (close/moderate/distant)

**Rhythm & Flow:**
- Pacing default (fast/moderate/leisurely)
- Variation strategy (when to speed up/slow down)
- Paragraph rhythm (short/long/mixed)
- White space usage

**Dialogue Approach:**
- Tag frequency (minimal/moderate/descriptive)
- Action beat usage
- Subtext level
- Naturalism vs. stylization

**Pattern Avoidance Guidelines:**
- Patterns to avoid (intensifiers, clichés, weak verbs)
- Maximum acceptable frequency per 1000 words
- Specific phrases to never use

**Example Passages:**
- Include 2-3 approved sample passages
- Annotate key voice elements
- Show voice in different scene types (action, dialogue, introspection)

### 7. Integration with Story Bible

Add voice profile to Story Bible:
- Link to prose sophistication setting from project config
- Reference genre and audience from SRD
- Include comp author references
- Embed example passages

### 8. Create Quick Reference Card

One-page summary for easy reference while writing:
- Voice identity statement
- Top 5 do's
- Top 5 don'ts
- 1-2 example sentences
- Comp author reminders

## Output
- Complete narrative voice profile document
- 3 sample passages (with 1 approved/refined version)
- Quick reference card for writers
- Updated Story Bible with voice section
- Voice profile saved to: `{output_folder}/narrative-voice-profile.md`

## Usage Notes

**When to use this workflow:**
- Early in planning phase, after SRD is complete
- Before writing first chapter
- When trying a new style or genre
- When collaborating with multiple writers who need consistency

**For experienced authors with established voice:**
- Can skip sample generation
- Focus on documenting existing voice for AI consistency
- Use as reference for voice matching workflow

**For new authors:**
- Sample generation is critical
- Experiment with multiple options
- Don't rush — voice is foundational
- Revisit after first chapter if needed

**Voice evolution:**
- Voice can evolve during drafting
- Update profile if significant changes occur
- Document intentional shifts
- Maintain core identity while allowing growth
