# Location Detail Workflow

## Objective
Develop a specific location in depth for vivid, immersive scene-setting.

## Steps

### 1. Core Identity
- What is this place? (building, landscape, city, room)
- What is its purpose in the story?
- What mood should it evoke?
- When do characters encounter it?

### 2. Physical Description
- Layout and spatial relationships
- Scale and dimensions (intimate room vs. vast landscape)
- Materials, textures, colors
- Light sources and quality of light
- State of repair or decay

### 3. Sensory Palette
- Sight: dominant visual impressions, colors, movement
- Sound: ambient noise, silence, echoes
- Smell: distinctive scents, pleasant or unpleasant
- Touch: temperature, air quality, surfaces
- Taste: if relevant (dust, salt air, cooking smells)

### 4. History and Context
- How old is this place?
- What happened here before the story?
- How has it changed over time?
- What does it mean to the people who use it?

### 5. Atmosphere and Mood
- Time of day and how it changes the feel
- Weather and seasonal effects
- How the location reflects or contrasts character emotions
- Hidden details that reward close attention

### 6. Character Interaction
- How do characters move through this space?
- What do they notice first?
- What details reveal character (what they focus on)?
- How does the location constrain or enable action?

### 7. Story Function
- What scenes take place here?
- How does the location serve the plot?
- Does it change over the course of the story?
- Symbolic significance (if any)

### 8. Document
- Add to Story Bible locations section
- Create sensory reference card for writing scenes here

## Output
- Detailed location profile
- Sensory reference card
- Updated Story Bible
