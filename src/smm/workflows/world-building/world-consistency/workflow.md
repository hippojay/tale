# World Consistency Check Workflow

## Objective
Verify world-building consistency across the entire manuscript.

## Steps

### 1. Load World Reference
- Load Story Bible world-building sections
- Note all established rules, facts, and details
- Create checklist of verifiable world elements

### 2. Geography and Distance
- Travel times consistent with established distances
- Locations described consistently across scenes
- Maps (if any) match narrative descriptions
- Seasonal and weather patterns logical

### 3. Magic/Technology Systems
- Rules established early are followed throughout
- Costs and limitations applied consistently
- No unexplained power escalation
- Characters don't forget abilities they've demonstrated

### 4. Timeline Consistency
- Day/night cycles make sense
- Seasons progress logically
- Character ages align with timeline
- Historical references consistent

### 5. Cultural Consistency
- Social customs applied uniformly
- Language and terminology consistent
- Power structures behave as established
- Economic details don't contradict

### 6. Object and Detail Tracking
- Named objects appear where they should
- Physical details of places don't shift
- Technology/tools used consistently
- Food, clothing, currency details stable

### 7. Cross-Chapter Verification
- Details introduced in one chapter honored in later ones
- No contradictions between character knowledge and world state
- Foreshadowed world elements pay off
- Background details don't shift without explanation

### 8. Document Findings
- List all inconsistencies with chapter references
- Categorize by severity (breaks story vs. minor detail)
- Recommend fixes for each issue
- Update Story Bible with corrections

## Output
- World consistency report
- Inconsistency list with locations and fixes
- Updated Story Bible
